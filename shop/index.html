Shen Pu-Er

Sheng Pu-Er (Raw Pu-Erh or Sheng Puerh): Also known as "raw" or "green" Pu-erh. This type of tea undergoes a natural fermentation process over time, but it doesn't go through the same intense fermentation process as the other type called Shu Pu-Er. The leaves are lightly processed and dried without the addition of heat or steam. As a result, Sheng Pu-Er typically has a light-green color and a woody, herbal flavor. It's known for its ability to age and gradually improve in taste over time.

Da Hong Pao

"Da Hong Pao" (大红袍) is a type of oolong tea originating from the mountainous regions of Fujian Province, China. The name translates to "Big Red Robe." Da Hong Pao oolong is cultivated on rocky slopes in the Wuyi Mountains, imparting a unique terroir to the tea.

Tie Guan Yin

"Tie Guan Yin" is a premium Chinese oolong tea known for its floral aroma and smooth, creamy taste. It originates from Anxi County, Fujian Province, China. The tea undergoes a specific processing method, and its flavor can range from buttery to sweet, depending on the oxidation level. The name, "Iron Goddess of Mercy," reflects its balanced and elegant flavor profile. Tie Guan Yin has a cultural legend associated with its discovery by a farmer near an iron statue of the goddess Guan Yin. It is often prepared using the Gongfu Cha method, and sourcing from reputable sellers is recommended for quality assurance.
